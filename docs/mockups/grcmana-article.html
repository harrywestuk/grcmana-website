Close Enterprise Deals

Every enterprise deal has a moment where the security question arrives. It might come in a vendor questionnaire, a legal review, or a casual ask from a CISO. For startups without a certification, that question ends the conversation. For those who have one, it accelerates it.

We've worked with over forty Seed-to-Series A startups on their first enterprise security programme, and the pattern is consistent: the companies that get certified fastest close the most deals. 01 · What the enterprise buyer actually sees

When a procurement team at a FTSE 250 or a regulated financial institution evaluates a vendor, the security review is not a formality. It is a structured risk assessment, often run by a dedicated InfoSec or third-party risk team with a standard questionnaire, a scoring model, and a minimum threshold. Below the threshold: no contract, no matter how good the product.

ISO 27001 certification is the most widely recognised signal that a vendor has a functioning information security management system — not just policies in a folder, but a live system with documented controls, trained staff, evidence logs, and external audit validation. For enterprise buyers, it compresses their risk review from weeks of back-and-forth to a single certificate check.

"We'd been stuck on the shortlist for three months. GRCMANA got us through ISO 27001 in six weeks and we closed the deal the following month. It wasn't paperwork — it was a commercial unlock."

02 · Why most startups wait too long

The default assumption is that certification is a heavy, expensive, months-long project — something you do after you've closed the enterprise deal, not before. That assumption is wrong on both counts.

A well-run ISO 27001 programme for a startup with 10–50 staff takes six to eight weeks. The cost, when structured properly, is a fraction of a single enterprise contract. The ROI calculation is not complicated: if one deal is worth £200k ARR and certification removes the blocker that was holding it up, the project pays for itself on close day.

  • Fear of complexity: Most consultants present certification as an IT project with dozens of mandatory controls. It's not. A scoped ISO 27001 programme for a SaaS startup has a manageable control set.
  • Believing the enterprise will wait: They won't. The shortlist moves on. Security shortcomings are rarely reconsidered once flagged.
  • Thinking it's a one-time thing: ISO 27001 is an ongoing management system. Building it right creates a repeatable, maintainable capability — not a one-off document exercise.

03 · The six-week certification path

The Trust Architecture Framework structures certification into four distinct phases, each with a clear deliverable. The key to the six-week timeline is sequencing — starting with scope and risk assessment in the first week, not at the end.

Trust Architecture Framework · Phase timeline Weeks 1–6 
// Phase 01 · DEFINE (Week 1)
scope:          Define ISMS boundary, identify assets
risk_register:  Identify threats, assess likelihood + impact
output:         "Risk Assessment Report + Statement of Applicability"

// Phase 02 · BUILD (Weeks 2–3)
controls:       Implement applicable Annex A controls
policies:       Draft and approve 12 core policy documents
output:         "Policy Pack + Control Evidence Baseline"

// Phase 03 · EMBED (Week 4)
training:       Staff awareness, role-specific briefings
evidence:       Begin evidence collection across control domains
output:         "Evidence Log + Internal Audit Report"

// Phase 04 · VALIDATE (Weeks 5–6)
stage_1_audit:  Document review with accredited certification body
stage_2_audit:  On-site / remote controls verification
output:         "ISO 27001:2022 Certificate"

This is not a template to apply unchanged. Scope is the most important decision you make in the first week — a poorly scoped ISMS creates significantly more work at every subsequent stage. The right scope for most Seed-to-Series A SaaS businesses is the product development and hosting environment, with a clearly defined boundary that excludes non-core systems.

04 · What happens after certification

A certificate is the beginning of the Trust Architecture, not the end. The companies that get the most commercial value from ISO 27001 treat it as a foundation: they add it to their website, their security page, their vendor questionnaire responses, and their sales decks. They also use it to accelerate the next framework — AI governance, EU AI Act readiness, SOC 2, or sector-specific requirements.

The Trust Maturity Model maps five stages from Exposed → Established → Controlled → Credible → Trusted. ISO 27001 certification moves a company from Established to Controlled. **The step to Credible** — the stage where enterprise deals stop requiring security reviews entirely because your reputation precedes you — requires consistent surveillance audit performance and visible trust signals across your go-to-market motion.
